Nashville Hot Chicken Sandwich

A crispy cornflake-crusted chicken thigh sandwich with a spicy Nashville-style hot sauce, served on a brioche bun with pickles. Recipe is per sandwich — multiply as needed. Only about 30g of breading sticks to each piece; measurements are sized to fill bowls with plenty of room to work.

Yield: 1 sandwich

```

Ingredients

Chicken

  • 170g trimmed chicken thigh

Bowl 1 — Seasoned Flour

  • 80g all-purpose flour
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Garlic powder, to taste
  • Paprika, to taste

Bowl 2 — Egg Wash

  • 1 large egg
  • 50g egg whites

Bowl 3 — Coating

  • 100g crushed cornflakes

Nashville Hot Sauce (makes enough for ~4 sandwiches)

  • 1/3 cup Frank's RedHot
  • Chilli powder, to taste
  • 1 tbsp yogurt butter or light butter
  • 20g brown sugar substitute
  • 1 tbsp honey

Garnish

  • 4–5 pickles
  • 1 brioche bun

Instructions

  1. Set up your three breading stations: Bowl 1 with flour, salt, pepper, garlic powder, and paprika; Bowl 2 with the egg and egg whites, whisked together; Bowl 3 with crushed cornflakes.
  2. Dredge the chicken thigh in Bowl 1 (seasoned flour), shaking off excess.
  3. Dip the floured chicken into Bowl 2 (egg wash), letting excess drip off.
  4. Press the chicken firmly into Bowl 3 (crushed cornflakes), coating thoroughly. About 30g of breading will stick.
  5. Cook the breaded chicken thigh using your preferred method (air fryer, oven, or fry) until cooked through and the crust is golden and crispy.
  6. While the chicken cooks, make the hot sauce: combine Frank's RedHot, chilli powder, butter, brown sugar substitute, and honey in a small saucepan over low heat. Stir until the butter melts and everything is combined.
  7. Brush or drizzle the hot sauce generously over the cooked chicken.
  8. Assemble: place the sauced chicken on the brioche bun and top with pickles. Serve immediately.

Nutrition

Nutrition information not specified.

```
